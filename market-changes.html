Exploring Market Changes Over Time

This section focuses on how real estate prices across Connecticut have changed year by year, and how those trends relate to broader economic indicators like inflation. By comparing housing data with the Consumer Price Index (CPI-U), we can observe patterns in how quickly home prices have risen or slowed down over time.

These visualizations are designed to highlight major shifts in the market and help users better understand how sale prices and assessed values compare to inflation across different years.

Median Sale Price vs. Median Assessed Value Over Time

The visualization below depicts the median sale prices of Connecticut real estate over the years, the median assessed values of those properties, and the difference between them. As you look at the visualization, youâ€™ll notice a bar between the two lines representing the difference between sale price and assessed value. A larger bar indicates a larger difference. Hover over the visualization to view tooltip information including the sale year, median assessed value ($), median sale value ($), and the difference between them ($).
Looking at the visualization above, we can see that the most commonly sold property type, across all years available (4/5/1999 - 8/31/2023), is Single Family homes, with a total of 401,612 sales. This is followed quickly by other residential properties. Unsurprisingly, the second least common property type sold is industrial real estate, with only 774 sales recorded. This makes sense considering most individuals aren't in a position to purchase industrial-grade properties, which are typically owned by businesses. Their high median sale price, as seen in the next visualization, also contributes to their limited number of transactions. You'll also notice "Public Utility" is the least common property type sold, with only 10 sales recorded. This is likely due to the fact that public utilities are typically owned/managed by the government, or there are limited public utility properties available for sale across the state.

Yearly Change in Median Sale Prices (Line Graph)

This helps highlight which types of properties are most popular, consistently in demand, and which may have experienced growth or decline in popularity over the years.
