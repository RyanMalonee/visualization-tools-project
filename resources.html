External Resources

Three of the visualizations throughout the project relied on CPI-U measurements to try to track the Year-Over-Year % change in inflation and deflation. Periods of inflation, deflation, and recessions play a vital role in almost any market, and the real estate market is no different. Although the CPI-U data only goes back as far as 2006, and the Connecticut data only goes back to 1999, the CPI-U data is still useful in understanding different trends that may be visible in the visualizations.

Two main events took place between 1999 and the present day, that led to periods of market downturn, or recessions. These include the COVID-19 pandemic, and the 2008 financial crisis. The 2008 financial crisis was a result of the housing bubble and subprime mortgage crisis, which led to a significant decline in housing prices and a large number of foreclosures. These two events had a significant impact on the real estate market in Connecticut, as well as the rest of the country. Because of these events' huge impact on different market, I wanted to highlight them in the visualizations, so that the viewer could see how the market reacted to these events.

To learn more about these two events, and their impact on markets across the country, check out these resources:
